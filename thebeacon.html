Introducing the Beacon

Test if your net leaks.

Hit the Lights

Put the Beacon in your network and warns if your net leaks. Spread them all around and show off how tight ship you are running.

Break out

Beacon emits test signals out of your network. Our service warns you if it detects changes in the ways they get out.

Hear Hear!

The Beacon (B) pings Beacon Service (BS). Beacon Service alerts you when your Internet access policy changes. You will only hear about them when we can hear them. >Read more.

Deploy Everywhere

The more complex your network is, the more valuable the Beacons are.

Appliance

Use the physical ones to test on premise locations. Leave them behind, move them around, have them all around. >Read more.

Virtual

Launch the virtual Beacons inside Cloud-services and server hotels and see if they can call home. >Read more.

Beacon Fits In

As Beacons do not need any special access, they are as easy to deploy as any other physical or virtual appliance.

Got a net? Have a Beacon!

There are many ways to isolate networks. LANs, Virtual LANs, VPNs/VPCs, MPLS. Put a Beacon in each of your networks and make sure they are not accidentally connected to Internet.

Carefree

Just Launch It!

Spawn and Spread

Beacon does not require complex configuration. Order a physical appliance from our service or create unique virtual machines to be spread around. Give your Beacons custom labels so that you'll know exactly where the alerts are coming from.

Replace

Update process is easy. You just swap the old Beacon with the new one. With the physical appliance you can just swap its SD-card.

Safe

Because security device demanding security exception is just silly.

One Way

The Beacon does not require inbound connections. You don't have to touch your access control in order to use them.

End-to-End Encrypted

Thanks to the Beacon's end-to-end encryption the receivers only know the ID's of your Beacons. The rest of the information is available only for you.

Your Disposable Heroes

Beacon lives in a read-only memory, which means you can dispose the mass media without worrying about leaks. Anything else would be just ironic, don't you think?
